I offer you two quality levels for AI image generation: **Pro** and **Ultra**. Each one is designed for a different scenario, and choosing the right one will let you make better use of your credits and get results that match exactly what you need.

Don't worry if you're not sure which to choose right away. I'll explain the differences, when to use each one, and how to combine them strategically to get the most out of your plan.

---

## The Two Levels

| Level      | Credits | Speed  | Quality   | Detail |
| ---------- | ------- | ------ | --------- | ------ |
| **Pro**    | 50      | Medium | Good      | Medium |
| **Ultra**  | 100     | Slower | Excellent | High   |

The main difference is the relationship between cost (credits), generation speed, and level of detail in the final result. Both levels produce images suitable for advertising, but Ultra takes the visual details to a superior level.

---

## Pro (50 credits)

### Characteristics

* **Speed:** Balanced -- you receive your image in seconds
* **Quality:** Good for most advertising uses
* **Detail:** Medium level, visible and coherent textures
* **Resolution:** High, sufficient for all platforms

### When to Use Pro

The Pro level is your daily workhorse. It's the perfect balance between quality and credit efficiency.

* **Regular daily use** -- weekly campaigns, creative tests, ad variations
* **Standard ads** -- most Instagram and Facebook Feed ads work perfectly with Pro quality
* **Balance between quality and cost** -- you get twice as many images for the same number of credits as Ultra
* **Most campaigns** -- for 8 out of 10 campaigns, Pro is more than sufficient

**Practical example:** If you have the PRO Plan with 400 credits, generating at Pro quality gives you up to 8 images per month. That's enough to test different creatives across 2 weekly campaigns.

---

## Ultra (100 credits)

### Characteristics

* **Speed:** Slower (worth the wait -- the AI dedicates more processing cycles to refining every detail)
* **Quality:** The best available on the platform
* **Detail:** Maximum level of detail -- hyperrealistic textures, cinematic lighting
* **Resolution:** Very high, perfect even for use outside social media

### When to Use Ultra

Ultra is for your most important moments, when the creative needs to shine and every detail counts.

* **Important campaigns** -- product launches, key seasons (Black Friday, Christmas), special events
* **High-end products** -- jewelry, fashion, premium technology, where visual detail is part of the value proposition
* **When detail is crucial** -- product close-ups, hero images for landing pages
* **Material for other uses** -- if you need to use the image on your website, in presentations, or in printed material

**Practical example:** You're launching your star product for the holiday season. In this case, it's worth investing 100 credits per image to get a creative that truly conveys the premium quality of your offer.

---

## How to Choose the Right Level

This quick guide will help you decide in each situation:

| Question | If the answer is "yes", use... |
| --- | --- |
| Is it a test campaign or A/B test? | **Pro** |
| Do I need several variations of the same concept? | **Pro** (maximize quantity) |
| Is it the main launch of a product? | **Ultra** |
| Is the product premium or luxury? | **Ultra** |
| Will I use this image across multiple channels? | **Ultra** |
| Will I only use it in Instagram/Facebook ads? | **Pro** is sufficient |

**Tip:** A smart strategy is to generate first in Pro to test different concepts and angles. Once you identify the winning concept, regenerate that image in Ultra for the final version of your campaign. This way you invest Ultra credits only on what you already know works.

---

## How Credits Are Consumed

Each generation deducts credits from your balance based on the chosen level (Pro: 50 credits, Ultra: 100 credits per image). You can see your credit balance on the generation screen before confirming, so you'll always know exactly how many images you can create with the credits you have left.

You have full control over how you distribute your credits between the two levels. There's no restriction on how many images you generate at each level.

---

## Visual Differences

So you understand what changes between one level and another, here's the detail for each visual aspect:

| Aspect            | Pro        | Ultra          |
| ----------------- | ---------- | -------------- |
| Textures          | Detailed   | Very detailed  |
| Lighting          | Realistic  | Photographic   |
| Edges             | Defined    | Very sharp     |
| Small details     | Present    | All visible    |
| Coherence         | Good       | Excellent      |

The most notable difference is in lighting and textures. In Ultra, reflective surfaces, fabrics, and product details look with a realism that approaches professional photography. In Pro, these elements are good but with a more contained level of detail.

## Recommendations

### Optimal Usage Strategy

1. **Regular use:** Use Pro for most of your daily needs -- proof of concept, ad variations, maintenance campaigns
2. **Final version:** For important campaigns or material you want to reuse across other channels, use Ultra

### Tips to Maximize Your Credits

1. **Plan which images you need** before you start generating -- avoid unnecessary generations
2. **Provide clear information** about what you want to create -- the more specific your description, the better the result on the first try
3. **Use Pro for daily use** (lower cost per image) and reserve Ultra for final versions
4. **Generate variations in Pro first**, choose your favorite, then create it in Ultra if you need maximum quality

## Frequently Asked Questions

<details>

<summary>Can I change the level after generating?</summary>

Not directly. You must generate again with the desired level. The information provided is preserved; only the quality changes. You don't need to re-describe what you want; simply select the other level and regenerate.

</details>

<details>

<summary>Do credits expire?</summary>

Credits included in your plan are renewed each billing cycle. Unused credits do not carry over to the next month, so I recommend using them before renewal.

</details>

<details>

<summary>Is the difference noticeable in an Instagram ad?</summary>

For most Feed ads, the difference is subtle because platforms compress images. Where Ultra really shines is in images with product close-ups, detailed backgrounds, or when you want to reuse the image on your website at a larger size.

</details>

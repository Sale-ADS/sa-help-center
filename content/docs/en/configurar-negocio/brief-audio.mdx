## What is the Brief?

The Brief contains:

- **Target audience** - Who you sell to
- **Value proposition** - What makes you unique
- **Goals** - What you want to achieve
- **Brand tone** - How you communicate
- **Key messages** - What you want to convey

**Professional advertising, extreme simplicity.** Instead of filling out lengthy forms, you simply **talk** and I extract all this information.

You have two options:

| Option | Description | Ideal for |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Record Audio** | Record directly from your browser | When you're in front of the computer |

## Option 1: Record Audio

### Questions You Should Answer

While recording, answer these questions:

1. **What products or services does your business offer?**
2. **Who is your ideal customer?** (age, interests, location)
3. **What main problem do you solve for your customers?**
4. **What makes your business unique compared to the competition?**

### Steps to Record

1. Click the **"Record Audio"** tab
2. Click **"Start Recording"**
3. Allow microphone access if prompted
4. Speak clearly answering the questions
5. Click **"Stop Recording"** when you're done

### Recording Requirements

| Requirement | Value |
|-------------|-------|
| **Minimum duration** | 10 seconds |
| **Maximum duration** | 3 minutes |
| **Format** | Automatic (WebM) |

<Callout type="info" title="Tip">
  Don't worry about being perfect. Speak naturally as if you were telling a friend about your business. **You have full control** over how you explain your business.
</Callout>


### After Recording

Once recorded:

1. You can **play** the audio to listen to it
2. If you don't like it, click **"Record Again"**
3. When you're satisfied, continue to the next step

## Common Errors

### "Audio too short"

**Message:** `Please record at least 10 seconds of audio to provide enough context.`

**Solution:** Record at least 10 seconds. Make sure to answer the guide questions.

### "Maximum duration reached"

**Message:** `The recording stopped automatically upon reaching 3 minutes.`

**Cause:** The recording reached the 3-minute limit.

**Solution:** This is not an error, the audio simply stopped. If you need to say more, you can continue in another recording or simply use what was recorded.

### "Could not access the microphone"

**Message:** `Please allow microphone access in your browser.`

**Solution:**
1. Click the lock icon in the address bar
2. Look for "Microphone" and select "Allow"
3. Reload the page and try again

### "File too large"

**Message:** `The audio file must not exceed 50MB.`

**Solution:** Compress the file or record a shorter one directly on the platform.

### "Invalid file"

**Message:** `Please select a valid audio file.`

**Solution:** Use one of the supported formats: MP3, WAV, M4A, OGG, WEBM.

## Audience Location

Selecting your service area (where you operate/deliver) is one of the most important steps in setting up your business.

The more specific your service area, the better I understand your market and the more relevant the suggestions I generate for you. Don't worry if you're not sure what to select; below you'll find a strategy guide based on your type of business.

---
### Why Is It Important?

The service area you select has a direct impact on three key aspects of your campaigns:

- **Where your ads are shown** (countries, cities, regions) -- defines the geographic reach of your advertising
- **What interests I suggest** (relevant to that market) -- the AI generates context-specific targeting for each region
- **What language and tone the copies use** (adapted to the region) -- writing for Colombia is not the same as writing for Spain or Mexico

<Callout type="warn" title="Important">
  I use your location to generate more relevant targeting interests. For example, if you select "Medellin", I will look for interests specific to the Colombian market.
</Callout>

This way I better understand your market and generate more refined targeting interests. This is one of the most powerful advantages of the platform: the AI doesn't just translate texts, it adapts the entire advertising strategy to the local context of your audience.

---
### Location Selection Strategy

Before you start searching for locations, think strategically about where your potential customers actually are. A good location selection can make the difference between a profitable campaign and one that wastes budget.

**Key principles:**

- **Less is more at the start** -- It's better to begin with a specific location and expand later than to cover an entire country from day one. A campaign focused on a single city learns faster what works.
- **Think about your service capacity** -- There's no point showing ads in cities where you can't deliver your product or provide your service.
- **Consider the cost per click** -- Larger cities tend to have more advertising competition and higher costs. Medium-sized cities sometimes offer a better cost-to-result ratio.

---
### How to Select Locations

#### Step 1: Search Locations

The search tool lets you find any location available in Meta's database:

1. In the search field, type the name of a country, city, or region
2. You must type at least 2 characters
3. A list of matching results will appear

**Search examples:**

- "Colombia" → Entire country
- "Bogota" → Specific city
- "California" → State/Region

You can choose exactly where to show your ads, with the granularity you need.

#### Step 2: Select from the List

Once you find the location you're looking for:

1. Click the desired location
2. The location will be added to your selected list
3. You can add multiple locations without limit

#### Step 3: Review Selection

Selected locations appear in the **"Selected locations"** section where you can review and adjust them:

- You can see all added locations in a clear list
- Click the **X** to remove a location you no longer want
- There is no location limit, but remember that more locations mean a broader audience and more distributed budget

You have full control of where your ads are shown. You can change the selection at any time.

---
### Location Types

You can select locations at three different levels of granularity, depending on your strategy:

| Type | Example | Reach | Best for |
|------|---------|-------|----------|
| **Country** | Colombia, Mexico, Spain | Entire country | National e-commerce, digital services |
| **State/Region** | Antioquia, California, Catalonia | Entire region | Regional businesses, franchises |
| **City** | Medellin, Mexico City, Barcelona | City and surroundings | Local businesses, in-person services |

<Callout type="info" title="Tip">
  For local businesses, select specific cities. For national e-commerce, select the entire country. And if you sell international digital services, you can select several Spanish-speaking countries.
</Callout>

---
### Recommendations by Business Type

Each type of business has an optimal location strategy. Use these recommendations as a starting point and adjust based on your results:

#### Local Business (Restaurant, Physical Store, Beauty Salon)

Your audience is within miles of your physical location. Focus on your real area of influence.

- Select your main city
- Add nearby cities if you make deliveries or if your customers travel to visit you
- Example: "Medellin", "Envigado", "Itagui"

#### National E-commerce

If you can ship anywhere in the country, take advantage of nationwide reach, but consider starting with the cities with the highest demand.

- Select the entire country if your logistics allow it
- Or the main cities where you have the best shipping coverage
- Example: "Colombia" or "Bogota", "Medellin", "Cali"

#### Digital Services / Online Consulting

Your service has no physical boundaries. You can reach anyone who speaks your language.

- You can select multiple Spanish-speaking countries
- Focus on markets where your service is most relevant
- Example: "Colombia", "Mexico", "Spain", "Argentina"

**Start where your best customers are and expand from there.**

---
### Important Information

#### Automatic Optimization

I automatically optimize locations to avoid conflicts and duplicates:

- **Remove duplicates** (if you select Colombia and Bogota, I only use Colombia because it already includes Bogota)
- **Group regions** when it makes sense to optimize delivery
- **Notify you of adjustments** made so you always know what I'm using

I explain in the interface what adjustments I make and why, so there are no surprises.

#### Change Location Later

If your business grows or you change markets, you can update your locations at any time:

1. Go to **Set Up Your Business**
2. Find the location section
3. Modify the selected locations

<Callout type="info" title="Note">
  Changing the location may require regenerating your Brief to update the suggested interests. This is because targeting interests are directly tied to the geography of your audience.
</Callout>

You can change your location whenever you want. Your business evolves, and the platform adapts with you.

---
### Common Errors

#### "Location required"

**Message:** `Please select at least one location before continuing.`

**Solution:** You must select at least one location to continue. Use the search tool to add one. Without a location, I cannot generate targeting interests or properly configure your campaigns.

#### "No results found"

**Possible causes:** The name doesn't match locations in Meta's database, or you typed too few characters.

**Solution:**

1. Check the spelling of the location name
2. Try with the name in English (some locations are cataloged in English in Meta's database)
3. Use a more generic name (country instead of a small city, or the name of the main city in the area)

---
### Why Select Now

The location is configured in this step because it is used throughout the entire platform flow:

1. **Generate the Brief** with relevant local interests for your market
2. **Configure strategies** automatically with the correct geographic targeting
3. **Optimize the targeting** of your ads with contextual data from the region

> **Example:** If you select "Medellin", the AI will look for interests like "Feria de las Flores", "Parque Arvi", "Metro de Medellin" that are relevant to that audience. If you select "Mexico City", the interests will be completely different and adapted to that market.

When you see how the AI automatically adapts the entire strategy to your specific location, you'll understand the true value of this seemingly simple step.

## What to Say in the Audio

### Example of a Good Audio

> "Hi, I have a sportswear store in Medellin called FitStyle. We sell clothing for gym, running, and yoga. My ideal customer is women aged 25 to 45 who exercise regularly and are looking for comfortable yet stylish clothing. What sets us apart is that all our clothing is made from high-quality Colombian fabrics with exclusive designs. The problem we solve is that women can't find sportswear that is both functional and elegant enough to wear outside the gym. Our tone is motivational and approachable."

### Key Points to Mention

- Your business name
- What you sell (products/services)
- Who you sell to (ideal customer)
- What makes you different
- What problem you solve
- How you want to be perceived (tone)

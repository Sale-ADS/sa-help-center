## Which Copies You Can Edit

Each ad has three editable fields. Each one serves a different purpose in the user's journey from seeing your ad to clicking:

### 1. Primary Text

* The main copy of your ad, the first thing your audience reads
* Appears above the creative
* The first 125 characters are visible without expanding
* You can include emojis, hashtags, mentions

**Recommendations:**

* Start with a hook that captures attention in the first 2 seconds of reading
* Include the main benefit you offer
* End with a clear call to action

### 2. Headline

* Short and eye-catching text that appears below the creative
* Maximum 40 characters recommended
* Should be direct, clear, and use an action verb

**Recommendations:**

* Use action verbs ("Discover", "Save", "Transform")
* Highlight the main benefit in few words
* Be specific: "Save 80% of your time" is better than "Improve your life"

### 3. Description

* Additional information that appears below the headline
* Maximum 90 characters recommended
* Complements the headline, doesn't repeat it

**Recommendations:**

* Add relevant details that reinforce the decision
* Mention offers, promotions, or differentiators
* Include urgency if applicable ("This week only", "Limited spots")

## How to Edit

### Step 1: Activate Editing

1. In the preview, click the **"Edit"** button
2. The text fields will become editable

### Step 2: Modify the Text

1. Click on the field you want to edit
2. Modify the text
3. Watch the character counter in real time

### Step 3: Save Changes

1. Click **"Save"**
2. Or click **"Cancel"** to discard changes

There's no pressure: you can edit, save, edit again, until it's perfect.

## Character Indicators

While editing, you'll see counters that guide you:

**Within the limit:**

```
85 characters remaining
```

**Exceeding the limit:**

```
-12 characters over the limit
```

<Callout type="info" title="Note">
  You can exceed the limit, but the text may be cut off in some placements (especially in Stories and Reels). If possible, stay within the limit to ensure your message is fully visible across all formats.
</Callout>

## Tips for Good Copies

### For the Primary Text

**Recommended structure:**

```
[Attention hook]
[Main benefit]
[Social proof or feature]
[Call to action]
```

**Example:**

> Tired of wasting time on your campaigns?
>
> With SaleAds.ai you create professional ads in minutes, not hours.
>
> +1000 businesses already trust our AI.
>
> Try free today →

### For the Headline

**Good examples:**

* "Save 80% of your time"
* "Results in 24 hours"
* "Limited time offer"

**Bad examples:**

* "Click here" (too generic)
* "The best product in the world" (not credible)

### For the Description

**Good examples:**

* "No credit card required"
* "Free shipping today"
* "Last units available"

## Using Emojis

Emojis can increase engagement when used with judgment. It's not about filling the text, but about accentuating key points:

**Where to use:**

* Beginning of the primary text (to capture visual attention)
* Before bullet points (to create visual structure)
* At the end of the CTA (to reinforce the action)

**Example:**

> Fire! Season sale!
>
> 50% off - Free shipping - 30-day guarantee
>
> Shop now

<Callout type="warn" title="Caution">
  Don't overuse emojis. 2-4 is enough. Too many make your ad look unprofessional and can reduce your audience's trust.
</Callout>

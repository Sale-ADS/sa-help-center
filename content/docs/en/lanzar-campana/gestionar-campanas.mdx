## Create a Campaign

Creating a campaign in SaleAds is a guided process where I accompany you at every step. Below you'll see how the preview works, which is the key moment before launching: where you check that everything looks exactly how you want it.



## Preview Campaigns

After uploading your creatives and having me generate the copies, one of the most important moments in the process arrives: seeing how your ads will look in the real world. The preview lets you review every detail before investing a single cent.

---

## The Preview Screen

After I generate the copies, you'll arrive at the **"Preview & Edit"** screen. Here you'll see everything your audience will see when your ad appears on their feed:

* Your creatives (images/videos)
* The copies I generated based on your Brief
* Preview of different placements (Feed, Stories, Reels)
* Editing options to adjust any detail

---

## Preview Elements

### Ad Information

Each ad shows the same elements your audience will see on Facebook and Instagram:

| Element                | Description                       |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| **Business name**      | Your page name                    |
| **Label**              | "Sponsored" (automatic from Meta) |
| **Creative**           | Your image or video               |
| **Primary text**       | Main ad copy                      |
| **Headline**           | Ad headline                       |
| **Description**        | Additional text                   |
| **CTA button**         | "Shop Now", "Contact", etc.       |

### How to Interpret the Preview

The preview is a faithful simulation of how your ad will look. Pay special attention to the **primary text**: the first 125 characters are what people see without clicking "See more". If your key message isn't in those first characters, many people will never read it.

### Simulated Interactions

The preview shows simulated metrics to provide visual context:

* Likes
* Comments
* Shares

<Callout type="info" title="Note">
  These numbers are examples to show how the post will look. They do not represent actual metrics or performance predictions.
</Callout>

## Preview Placements

You can see how your ad will look in different formats. Each placement has its own characteristics, and your creative automatically adapts to each one:

### Feed

* Normal post view in the timeline
* 1:1 (square) or 4:5 (vertical) aspect ratio
* Text visible before "See more"

### Reels

* Vertical 9:16 format, full screen
* Ideal for videos with movement and energy
* Text in overlay on the video

### Stories

* Full vertical screen
* Short duration, immediate impact
* Disappears in 24 hours, creates urgency

### Carousel (if applicable)

* Multiple images/videos in sequence
* User swipes to see more
* Each slide can have its own copy

---

## Character Information

When editing copies, you'll see counters that help you keep your texts within optimal limits:

```
125 characters remaining
```

Or if exceeded:

```
-15 characters over the limit
```

### Recommended Limits

| Field           | Recommended Limit                      |
| --------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Primary text    | 125 characters (visible without "See more") |
| Headline        | 40 characters                          |
| Description     | 90 characters                          |

<Callout type="info" title="Tip">
  The first 125 characters of the primary text are the most important. They're what your audience sees before deciding whether they want to read more. Make sure your hook is there.
</Callout>

## Navigate Between Creatives

If you uploaded multiple creatives, each one has its own personality and copy:

* Use the arrows to navigate between creatives
* Or click on the indicators
* Each creative has its own set of copies

**Example:**

```
Video #1 | Video #2 | Video #3
```

Review each one individually. One creative may look perfect while another needs adjustments.

</Callout>

## What to Verify in the Preview

Before continuing, use this mental checklist. Each point can make the difference between an ad that converts and one that goes unnoticed:

* [ ] The primary text conveys your message in the first 125 characters
* [ ] The headline is attractive, clear, and uses an action verb
* [ ] The description complements the headline without repeating it
* [ ] The creative looks good in all placements (Feed, Stories, Reels)
* [ ] There are no spelling or grammar errors
* [ ] The CTA is appropriate for your objective (Shop Now, Contact, Learn More)
